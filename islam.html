Islam

Islam is a monotheistic religion. It's based on the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad Sallallahu Alaihi Wasallam.

Islam was originated in Mecca and Medina in the 7th century CE. It is one of the fastest-growing religions in the world.

Monotheism (Tawhid)

Muslims believe there is only one God (called Allah in Arabic), who is most merciful, all-knowing, and also all-powerful.

This belief is the first and most important pillar of Islam. We (Muslims) call this concept Tawhid.

The Quran

The Quran is the holy book of Islam. Muslims believe it's the literal word of God as revealed to Prophet Muhammad Sallallahu Alaihi Wasallam over a 23-year period.

The holy book was written in Arabic and is the best source of guidance for Muslims with regards to morals, faith, and daily life.

Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W)

Muslims believe that God sent Prophet Muhammad Sallallahu Alaihi Wasallam as the final prophet to mankind.

He is considered as the "Seal of the Prophets" and his teachings, actions, and words, known as Hadiths, provide guidance for Muslims to live a righteous life.

Five Pillars of Islam:

  • Shahada (Faith): The Shahada is an oath of faith that reads, "There is no god but Allah, and Prophet Muhammad Sallallahu Alaihi Wasallam is the Messenger of Allah."
  • Salat (Prayer): Muslims are obligated to pray five times a day facing the Kaaba in Mecca. These prayers are performed at a specific time and follow set gestures and recitations.
  • Zakat (Charity): Muslims must donate a certain percentage of their money, usually 2.5% annually, to help those in need.
  • Sawm (Fasting): Muslims fast from sunrise to sunset every day in the holy month of Ramadan. Muslims refrain from food, drinks, and other physical needs as an act of worship and self-control.
  • Hajj (Pilgrimage): Muslims who are physically and financially able must perform Hajj at least once in their lifetime. Mecca is the location of this pilgrimage.

Islamic Law (Sharia)

Sharia is the Islamic law that comes from the Quran and Hadith. It includes many aspects of life, such as law, ethics, and morals. It provides guidelines for family, business, and other areas.

Prophets

Muslims believe and honor all of the prophets listed in the Quran, including Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and many others.

Hereafter

Muslims believe in the Day of Judgment, when everyone will be held accountable for their deeds. Those who have lived righteously will be rewarded with paradise, and those who have done wrong will face punishment.

Community (Ummah)

Muslims are encouraged to take part in a global community, helping and guiding other Muslims while pursuing peace and righteousness.
